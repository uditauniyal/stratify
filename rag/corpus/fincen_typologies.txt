FINCEN MONEY LAUNDERING TYPOLOGIES
==================================

1. STRUCTURING (SMURFING)
-------------------------
Activity Code: S

Indicators:
- Multiple transactions conducted below the $10,000 Currency Transaction Report (CTR) threshold.
- Multiple branches visited in a short timeframe to deposit cash.
- Multiple individuals making cash deposits into a single account.
- Breaking large cash amounts into smaller, less conspicuous sums.
- Deposits frequently ranging between $9,000 and $9,900.
- Consistent patterns of cash deposits with no clear business purpose.

Red Flags:
- Cash deposits consistently just below reporting thresholds ($10,000).
- Multiple branch visits on the same day.
- Customer appears nervous or reluctant to provide identification.
- Customer inquiries about reporting thresholds.
- Sudden change in deposit behavior inconsistent with stated occupation.

2. LAYERING
-----------
Activity Code: L

Indicators:
- Rapid movement of funds through multiple accounts to obscure the audit trail.
- Wire transfers to high-risk jurisdictions with weak AML controls.
- Conversion of cash into monetary instruments (cashier's checks, money orders) and vice versa.
- Use of shell companies or nominees to distance funds from their origin.
- Funds received and almost immediately transferred out to another account.
- Complex web of transactions with no apparent economic or business logic.

Red Flags:
- Complex, illogical patterns of fund transfers designed to obscure origin.
- Use of multiple intermediary accounts or "pass-through" accounts.
- Rapid movement of funds (velocity) preventing funds from settling.
- Geographic dispersion of transactions inconsistent with business operations.
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions or known tax havens.

3. FUNNEL ACCOUNT (MONEY MULE)
------------------------------
Activity Code: F

Indicators:
- Recently opened account showing sudden, unexplained high volume of activity.
- Deposits from multiple unrelated individuals in different geographic locations.
- Immediate withdrawal of substantially all deposited funds, often via cash or wire.
- Account holder demographics (e.g., student, unemployed) inconsistent with transaction volume.
- No legitimate business purpose for the account activity.
- Account acts as a temporary repository for funds before they are moved elsewhere.

Red Flags:
- Account open for less than 6 months with high velocity activity.
- Deposit-to-withdrawal ratio nearing 1:1, leaving minimal balance.
- Cash withdrawals immediately following electronic deposits.
- Multiple unique depositors with no apparent connection to the account holder.
- Activity concentrated in a short timeframe, appearing bursty.

4. TRADE-BASED MONEY LAUNDERING (TBML)
--------------------------------------
Activity Code: T

Indicators:
- Over-invoicing or under-invoicing of goods and services.
- Multiple invoices for the same shipment of goods.
- Shipments to high-risk countries or free trade zones not associated with the business.
- Payments inconsistent with the type of business or stated purpose.
- Discrepancies between the description of goods on the invoice and the actual goods shipped.
- Use of complex trade financing arrangements to obscure the true nature of transactions.

Red Flags:
- Unusual payment patterns, such as third-party payments for goods.
- Payments to unrelated countries or entities not involved in the trade deal.
- Prices significantly above or below market value for the goods involved.
- Documentation appears altered, forged, or inconsistent.
- Transactions involving dual-use goods or restricted items.

5. CONTINUING ACTIVITY
----------------------
Activity Code: C

Indicators:
- Subject is the focus of a prior Suspicious Activity Report (SAR).
- Similar or escalating pattern of suspicious activity observed over time.
- Opening of new accounts or use of new methods to continue the same behavior.
- Activity shifting to different branches or jurisdictions to avoid detection.
- Persistence of red flags despite previous account closures or restrictions (if applicable and strictly internal).
- Continued interactions with known bad actors or high-risk entities.

Red Flags:
- Prior Document Control Number (DCN) on file for the same subject.
- Pattern matches or escalates typology identified in previous SARs (within 12-24 months).
- Subject modifies methods (e.g., changes branches, lowers amounts) to avoid detection.
- Activity volume or velocity increases after a period of dormancy.
- Re-emergence of subject after previous account closure or investigation.
